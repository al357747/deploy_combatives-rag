# Knees and teeps

**Purpose:** Teeps are long range straight line attack that can stop the forward motion of your opponent or knock them backward off their feet.  Knees are shorter range straight line attacks that can be used to supplement punches to enter clinch or be used while clinching.

## Long knee

Josh provided basic instructions on the long knee.  The arms go up above the head like about to throw a soccer ball in bounds. He then showed how to throw the rear forward, making sure to point your toes in to flare out your ankles. The knee should come up like a spear to your opponent's body. Your arms should stay up to protect your head with the arm on the same side of the kneeing leg going down just a little further to facilitate hip extension.

## Teep

Josh then showed how to throw a front kick also known as a teep. He showed how to throw a front teep And a rear teep. The rear teep is also referred to as a push kick.  In Jaguar Muay Thai, Josh calls A lead leg front kick as a teep and a rear front kick as a push kick. Josh then showed combinations involving teeps and round kicks.
